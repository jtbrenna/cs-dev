Abstract

 

Many future NASA missions will be accomplished by deploying multiple spacecraft.  These Distributed Space Systems (DSS) allows scientists to exploit new vantage points, develop new sensing strategies and implement system-wide techniques that promote agility, adaptability, evolvability, scalability, and affordability.  They will enable scientific returns that are not possible by flying single-spacecraft missions.  Information technology has played an important role since the beginning of space flight and will take on an ever more central role in DSS missions. Significant advances in information technology research will be required to meet the challenges posed by the DSS mission.  The Autonomous Nano-Technology Swarm (ANTS) is an advanced, biologically-inspired, DSS mission concept that builds on the recent advances in the field of robotics, artificial intelligence, and distributed systems technologies.  I will illustrate the information technology challenges by describing the Prospecting ANTS Mission to explore the Main Belt Asteroids (http://ants.gsfc.nasa.gov).
