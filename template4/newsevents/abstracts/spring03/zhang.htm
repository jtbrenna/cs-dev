Abstract

 

Image reconstruction is an inverse problem consisting of the identification of the input of a system given the knowledge of its output. The approximate extended Kalman filter (AEKF) has been suggested as an appropriate inverse method for reconstructing fluorescent properties in large tissue samples from frequency domain data containing measurement errors. It seeks to minimize the predicted error variances of the estimated optical properties in relation to measurement and system errors. Inverse problems are generally ill-posed. Furthermore, the computational overhead is very large for the full AEKF algorithm when applied to large datasets. In order to reconstruct a 3D image with high resolution, we developed three hybrid forms of the AEKF algorithm that may improve computational performance. The computational complexity and convergence of algorithms were analyzed. The numerical results of image reconstruction from actual data show that one hybrid form of the AEKF outperforms the traditional full AEKF in both image quality and computational efficiency in two test cases.
