JMTk and GenRC: A New Infrastructure and a New Algorithm for Memory Management

 

Dr. Steve Blackburn

Department of Computer Science

Australian National University

 

Date: Monday, April 28, 2003

Time: 12:20 p.m. - 1:10 p.m.

Location: 367 Votey

 

Abstract

 

This talk addresses new work in memory management, through the design of a new algorithm that tackles a pressing performance issue, and through the implementation of new infrastructure that meets an important research need. First I will discuss the design and implementation of JMTk, the new memory management subsystem of the Jikes RVM.  The goals of JMTk are to provide an efficient, composable, extensible, and portable toolkit for quickly building and evaluating new and existing garbage collection algorithms.  The project was an interesting exercise in abstraction and software engineering, and was underpinned from the outset by the intensely pragmatic objective of providing an environment for rapid, credible exposition of new ideas in memory management.  I will briefly discuss current efforts to port JMTk to other runtimes, including the OVM project, ghc (the Glasgow Haskell Compiler), and Microsoft's Rotor. The remainder of the talk will focus on GenRC, a new garbage collection algorithm that combines generational collection with reference counting to effectively tackle both throughput and responsiveness.  This project was motivated by dissatisfaction with a dichotomy in the GC community that tends to see throughput and responsiveness as mutually exclusive objectives.  It is alarmingly clear that this position is untenable as heaps grow larger, to the point now where collections of very large heaps in popular production VMs can sometimes take seconds.  I will explain our generalization of deferred reference counting, the key algorithmic insight that allows this otherwise obvious hybrid to work, and discuss our results that show how effectively we have been able to meet both throughput and responsiveness objectives.

 

(JMTk is joint work with Perry Cheng of IBM Research and Kathryn McKinley of the University of Texas at Austin and GenRC is joint work with Kathryn McKinley.)

 

Short biography

 

Steve Blackburn is a Senior Lecturer at the Australian National University.  He recently completed a post-doc at the University of Massachusetts.  He is a member of the Jikes RVM core team and co-developer and maintainer of JMTk.  His primary research focus for the past few years has been on understanding how the modern programming languages can address the looming "memory wall" through their memory management, compiler, and runtime systems. Previous research interests include scalability, databases and orthogonal persistence, and parallel and distributed computing.
