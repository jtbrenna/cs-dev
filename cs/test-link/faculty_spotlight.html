Dr. Josh Bongard

Assistant Professor Josh Bongard was recently awarded the prestigious 2007 Microsoft Research New Faculty Fellowship.

His research centers on evolutionary robotics, evolutionary computation, and physical simulation. In fact, you can help with his current research exploring the nature of aesthetic preference by answering a few questions: Go now!

To find out more about Dr. Bongard's work, peruse his website, read an interview with Josh, find out about his research on self-healing robots that aired on the Discovery Channel, or read his new book, How the Body Shapes the Way We Think: A New View of Intelligence.


Dr. Christian Skalka

Assistant Professor Christian Skalka's research is supported by grants from the Air Force Office of Scientific Research and NASA.

His work focuses on programming languages, especially languages such as Java that support secure distributed computing. Read his magazine article on this topic.

More recently, Dr. Skalka's attention has turned to wireless sensor network (WSN) programming. One current project is an interdisciplinary effort to provide low-cost WSN technology for monitoring the amount of water in snowpack for hydrological forecasting; see a trip report on recent field work. Dr. Skalka also provides research opportunities for undergraduates in WSN programming.

For many more details about his work, visit Dr. Skalka's website.


Dr. Xindong Wu

Xindong Wu is a Professor and the Chair of the Department of Computer Science at the University of Vermont.

Dr. Wu holds a PhD in Artificial Intelligence from the University of Edinburgh, Britain. His research interests include data mining, knowledge-based systems, and Web information exploration. He has published over 160 refereed papers in these areas in various journals and conferences, including IEEE TKDE, TPAMI, ACM TOIS, DMKD, KAIS, IJCAI, AAAI, ICML, KDD, ICDM, and WWW, as well as 18 books and conference proceedings.

In addition, Dr. Wu won the 2005 IEEE ICTAI Best Paper Award and the 2007 IEEE ICDM Best Theory/Algorithms Paper Runner Up Award.

For more details about his work, visit Dr. Wu's website.


Senior Lecturer Robert M. Erickson, MS

Bob received the prestigious 2007 Kroepsch-Maurice Teaching Award, having joined the Computer Science faculty in 1991 after completing his Master of Science degree at Clarkson University.

Bob focuses on a series of web technology courses that get students to think of the web not as a series of web pages, but rather as a user interface for computer programs (see this tree identification example). Students' projects range from blogs, hotel management and social networking sites to volunteer opportunities (having been a mentor for seven years, Bob likes to point students in this direction).

In addition, Bob has written a textbook on Microsoft Office suite that students have found useful not just for the course but, based on the emails Bob receives, for years to come. One former student emailed, "I still apply many of your lessons today — still have your old textbook in my office — do you have a new one?" The reply — it's online, of course: 2003 version | 2007 version.

For more information, visit Bob's website.
