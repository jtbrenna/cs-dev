Abstract

 

Modeling the spatial patterns of ecological and socio-economic processes and changes over time at different scales is critical to effective environmental management. I will describe some possible approaches to integrated spatial modeling with illustrations based primarily on the Patuxent watershed model that I have been working on during the past 6 years. The Patuxent Landscape Model (PLM) was built to simulate fundamental ecological processes on the watershed scale, in interaction with an economic component that predicts the land use patterns.  Local ecosystem dynamics are replicated across a grid of cells that compose the rasterized landscape.  Different habitats and land use types translate into different parameter sets to be fed into the modules describing local processes.  Cells are linked by spatial hydrologic modules that define horizontal fluxes of material and information. The model is complex and we face a number of challenges in running, calibrating and analyzing the model.
