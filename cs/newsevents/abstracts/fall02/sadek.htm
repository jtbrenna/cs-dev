Abstract

 

While information technology has facilitated the collection of never-before-seen quantities of data, this data has not always provided the information needed by transportagtion professionals to support sound decision-making.  One paradigm that holds high potential to support the needs of transportation professionals is Computational Intelligence (CI).  CI has resulted from a synergy among information processing technologies such as Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs), fuzzy sets, Case-Based Reasoning (CBR), genetic algorithms (GA’s), and Simulated Annealing (SA).  In the current presentation, we describe the use of CI paradigms to solve two complex transportation problems, namely: (1) the dynamic route guidance problem; and (2) the problem of deriving land use limits from infrastructure capacity.  For the dynamic route guidance problem, a hybrid CBR/SA system was developed for on-line traffic diversion, whereas a modular ANN was developed for deriving land use limits from infrastructure capacity.  Simulation studies were conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of the CI paradigms in solving the aforementioned two problems.
