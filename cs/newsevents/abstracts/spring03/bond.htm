A model of DNA sequence evolution in which substitution rates depend on DNA sequence context

 

Dr. Jeff Bond

Department of Microbiology and Molecular Genetics

University of Vermont

 

Date: Friday, April 18, 2003

Time: 12:20 p.m. - 1:10 p.m.

Location: 367 Votey

 

Abstract

 

It is usual to model DNA sequence evolution using a Markov model in which the rate of substitution at any position in the sequence does not depend on the identity of other characters in the sequence (i.e. the sequence context).  It is understood, however, that substitution rates do depend on DNA sequence context.  We will present estimates of context-dependent substitution rates in primates based on a model in which substitution rates depend on adjacent characters and on a simplified statistical method.  We will then describe a Markov Chain Monte Carlo method for statistical estimation that accurately samples based on the model and will describe preliminary results towards implementing it.
