Mining Both Positive and Negative Association Rules

 

Dr. Xindong Wu

Department of Computer Science

University of Vermont

 

Date: Friday, February 14, 2003

Time: 12:20 p.m. - 1:10 p.m.

Location: 367 Votey

 

Abstract

 

Association rules are traditionally defined as implications of the form A => B, where A and B are frequent item sets in a transaction database. In this talk, we extend this definition to include association rules of forms A => ~B, ~A => B, and ~A => ~B, which indicate negative associations between item sets. We call rules of the form A => B positive rules, and rules of the other forms negative rules. Negative rules are also very useful in association analysis, although they are hidden and different from positive rules. We present a method that mines both positive and negative rules. The negative rules are generated from infrequent item sets.
