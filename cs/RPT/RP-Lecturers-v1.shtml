Reappointment and Promotion Guidelines for Lecturers and Senior Lecturers

Last modified: .

1. Introduction

In accordance with the Agreement Between the University of Vermont and United Academics (AAUP/AFT) (referred to as the Union Contract hereafter), this document is developed to provide reappointment and promotion guidelines for Lecturers and Senior Lecturers in the Department of Computer Science. The Department applies the quality criteria for teaching, advising and service in the Evaluation of Faculty and Reappointment, Promotion and Tenure Criteria and Procedures in the Union Contract (Article 15), and has the following additional specific descriptions.

2. Student Selection for Teaching and Advising Evaluations

  1. Numbers of students and advisees to be contacted (N).

    The Chair will solicit teaching evaluations from N students and advising evaluations also from N advisees, where N is as follows for the following types of RPT reviews: 

      N: Type of review
---  --------------------------------------------------------------------
  4: Lecturer/Senior Lecturer re-appointments with green sheet reviews
  6: Senior Lecturer promotions
  2. The candidate will be asked to provide a list of N nominations for students and another list of N nominations for advisees.

  3. An ad-hoc committee will be formed each academic year by the Chair to provide N student nominations and N advisee nominations:

    1. The ad-hoc committee will consist of at least two members;
    2. The Department Office will provide (i) the candidate's student lists with grades since the candidate's last greensheet review, and (ii) the candidate's current advisee list;
    3. The ad-hoc committee will compile an initial list of at least 1.5*N students and another initial list of at least 1.5*N advisees from (b); and
    4. The candidate will be given a chance to look at these initial lists and cross out up to 0.5*N students from each list with reasons.

    To maintain consistency, the ad hoc committee will be formed each academic year for all RPT candidates.

  4. The Chair will select N students and N advisees from (2) and (3.d). These students and advisees will be contacted by both e-mail and registered mail, and the Chair will provide a memo in the candidate's greensheets on how the students and advisees are selected.

In the event that the same student is selected to evaluate both teaching and advising, he/she may write a single letter that addresses both teaching and advising.

3. Peer Teaching Evaluations

For each greensheet review, 2 to 3 faculty members will be invited by the Chair to provide peer teaching evaluations. The candidate is allowed to name up to 2 faculty members in the Department who should not be invited for this purpose.

The peer teaching evaluations should come from qualified faculty, and some of them should come from eligible voters as defined in Section 5.2 if at all possible. For a candidate who teaches a service course, the Chair is encouraged to invite an appropriate faculty member from another department to provide a peer teaching evaluation.

The peer evaluators are advised to look over the candidate's course materials as well as attend at least one of the candidate's lectures.

4. Advising

Lecturers and Senior Lecturers under greensheet reviews are advised to have a separate section on advising in their greensheets. In addition to student numbers, useful information would include:
  1. How often the candidate meets with his/her students.
  2. How actively the candidate contacts them when they need the advisor's help.
  3. Which type of feedback the candidate gets from them (relevant correspondence would be useful to mention).
  4. Whether the candidate has helped the Department in providing advising help.

5. Faculty Input and Eligible Voters for RPT Reviews

5.1. Faculty Input and Schedule for RPT Reviews

The Chair should set an appropriate schedule for each greensheet review, so that the complete greensheets will be ready for faculty review at least 2 weeks before the submission deadline to the Dean's Office.

Once the greensheets are ready for faculty review, all faculty members, tenured and untenured (including tenure-track/tenured faculty, research faculty, Lecturers, and Senior Lecturers) will be invited to review the greensheets and share their advice concerning the candidate with the Chair within a week. The feedback will be documented in the Chair's Evaluation.

At the beginning of the second week after the greensheets are complete, the Chair will convene (i) a meeting of all faculty members to discuss the greensheets, and (ii) a closed session for all eligible voters (as defined in Section 5.2) to vote on whether or not to recommend the candidate's application. This vote will be recorded in the Chair's Evaluation.

After the above faculty feedback and eligible voters' vote, the Chair will decide whether or not to recommend the candidate's application, and will inform the candidate with a detailed Chair's Evaluation.

5.2. Eligible Voters for Lecturer/Senior Lecturer Greensheet Reviews

  • For a Lecturer reappointment with a greensheet review, Senior Lecturers, tenure-track/tenured faculty members, and those Lecturers who have successfully passed a reappointment review in the past and are not applying for a reappointment in the current year, are eligible voters.
  • For a Senior Lecturer application, only Senior Lecturers and tenured faculty members are eligible voters.
  • For a Senior Lecturer reappointment with a greensheet review, only tenured faculty members are eligible voters.
The Chair is not an eligible voter.

6. Guidelines for Promotion to Senior Lecturer

The following guidelines for promotion to Senior Lecturer were formally approved by Dean Robert Jenkins on May 29, 2003.

An application for Senior Lecturer in Computer Science will be evaluated on the following criteria.

  • A minimum of 6 years (within an eight year period) of service, as specified in the Union Contract
  • Subject to a regular RPT review without the research expectations
  • A good citizenship in terms of service activities within the Department
  • Evidence of sustained quality teaching

7. Rebuttal and Appeal

After receiving the Chair's Evaluation on a reappointment or promotion review, the faculty member may rebut or appeal, following the procedures described in the Union Contract.
