1. Introduction

In accordance with the Agreement Between the University of Vermont and United Academics (AAUP/AFT) (referred to as the Union Contract hereafter), this document is developed to provide RPT (reappointment, promotion and tenure) and annual review guidelines in the Department of Computer Science.

Xindong Wu (the Chair) wrote the first draft of this document, and sent it to all Computer Science faculty for feedback on February 24, 2004. A revised version after individual faculty feedback will be discussed at a faculty meeting on March 4, 2004, before the final version is sent to the Dean for approval.

2. Department Considerations

The Department of Computer Science is a research-oriented department. The Department upholds and strengthens the mission of the University of Vermont towards the provision of innovation in research and scholarship, excellence in instruction, and public service to the citizens of the state, nation and world. The Department aspires to be a center of excellence in teaching computer science at all levels, emphasizing both long-term academic preparation and shorter-term economic importance; and as a center for excellence in research and graduate education by developing strengths in a small number of focused research areas and by exploiting Computer Science's unique opportunities for collaborations with other strong research areas in the University.

The Department is moving forward at a fast pace with our PhD program, and aims to become a first-rate Computer Science department in the near future. We expect each faculty member (except non-tenure-track research faculty) to be an effective teacher, and all research faculty and tenure-track faculty members to be active researchers in their respective areas.

3. RPT Evaluations in Computer Science

For reappointment, promotion and tenure (RPT) evaluations, the Department applies the Criteria in Appointment, Reappointment, Promotion, and Tenure Decisions in the University of Vermont Officers' Handbook and the criteria in the Officers of Instruction section in the Union Contract, and has the following additional specific descriptions. (See Appendix B below for a copy of the Criteria in Appointment, Reappointment, Promotion, and Tenure Decisions in the University of Vermont Officers' Handbook.)

3.1 Student Selection for Teaching Evaluations

The following procedure was discussed at a faculty meeting on 12/4/2003, and was emailed by the Chair to all faculty on 12/6/2003. 

 1. Number of students/advisees to be contacted (N)

Starting in January 2004, the Chair will contact the following numbers
of students (including advisees) for the following types of RTP
reviews:

  6: Lecturer/Senior Lecturer re-appointments with green sheet reviews
  6: First tenure-track re-appointments
 10: Senior Lecturer promotions and second tenure-track re-appointments
 12: Tenure and Associate/Full Professor promotions

 2. The candidate will be asked to provide N nominations.

At least 1/2 of these N nominations should also be the candidate's
former or present advisees.  When the candidate forwards these
nominations to the Chair, the advisees should be explicitly
identified.

 3. An ad-hoc committee will be formed each academic year by the Chair
    to provide another N nominations:

(a) The ad-hoc committee will consist of at least two members;

(b) The Department Office will provide (i) the student lists with
    grades from the candidate's last 6 years' classes, and (ii) the
    candidate's current advisee list;

(c) The ad-hoc committee will compile an initial list of at least
    1.5*N students from (b); and

(d) The candidate will be given a chance to look at this initial list
    and cross out up to 0.5*N students from the initial list.

To maintain consistency, the ad hoc committee will be formed each
academic year for all RTP candidates.

 4. The Chair will select 0.5*N students from the candidate's list and
    0.5*N students from the ad-hoc committee's list.

3.2 Peer Teaching Evaluations

For each greensheet review, 2 to 3 faculty members will be invited by the Chair to provide peer teaching evaluations. The candidate is allowed to name up to 2 faculty members in the Department who should not be invited for this purpose.

The peer teaching evaluations should come from eligible voters as defined in Section 3.6.2 if at all possible. For a candidate who teaches a service course, the Chair is encouraged to invite an appropriate faculty member from another department to provide a peer teaching evaluation.

3.3 Advising

All teaching faculty under greensheet reviews are advised to have a separate section on advising in their greensheets, to provide information on their undergraduate, graduate, and postdoc supervision. In addition to student numbers, useful information would include:
  1. How often the candidate meets with his/her students.
  2. How actively the candidate contacts them when they need the advisor's help (e.g., when they get warning letters).
  3. Which type of feedback the candidate gets from them (relevant thank-you messages would be useful to include).
  4. Whether the candidate has helped the Department in providing advising help.
  5. Possible joint publications with the students and postdocs.

3.4 Research

The University RTP criteria (see Appendix B) require "evidence that the candidate is continuously and effectively engaged in scholarly activity of high quality and significance."
3.4.1 Refereed Conference Publications
Refereed conference publications are very important in many computer science areas, and getting accepted by a top-tier conference is difficult. All research, tenure-track and tenured faculty members are strongly encouraged to publish refereed articles in both journals and conferences.

When listing conference publications for RPT reviews, the candidate is asked to distinguish fully-refereed papers from non-refereed and abstract-refereed ones, and provide information about the conference acceptance rates if possible.

3.4.2 Interdisciplinary Research
The Department encourages and supports interdisciplinary research and collaborations with researchers from other departments and other institutions. Meanwhile, all tenured and tenure-track faculty members in the Department should establish themselves as computer scientists, and strong relevance to computer science is expected from their interdisciplinary research.

When listing interdisciplinary publications for RPT reviews, the candidate is advised to provide information about the relevance of the journals and conferences to computer science. Such information may include, but is not limited to:

  • How many other computer scientists have also published in the same journal (or conference proceedings), and how often?
  • When and where has the journal (or conference proceedings) been cited or ranked as a computer science journal?
  • Have many computer scientists cited articles from the journal (or conference proceedings)?
3.4.3 E-mail Addresses for All Co-Authors
For the following greensheet reviews,
  • second re-appointment for years 6 and 7 of tenure-track faculty,
  • tenure and promotion to Associate Professor,
  • promotion to the rank of Professor, and
  • promotion to Research Associate Professor and Research Professor,
each candidate will be asked to provide a list of e-mail addresses for all co-authors. Co-authors will be invited by the Chair to comment on their perception of the role of the candidate in their joint work.

3.5 Selection of Arm's-Length Evaluators

For the following greensheet reviews, "arm's-length" evaluators will be solicited to provide external reports:
  • second re-appointment for years 6 and 7 of tenure-track faculty,
  • tenure and promotion to Associate Professor,
  • promotion to the rank of Professor, and
  • promotion to Research Associate Professor and Research Professor.
Arm's-length evaluators are individuals who do not have a significant personal relationship with the candidate. They are not former students, thesis advisors, formal or present colleagues, co-authors, or collaborators.

Also, each arm's-length evaluator should

  1. Be tenured in a recognized Computer Science department (or similar), or very well-known industry-based researchers; and for promotions to the ranks of Professor and Research Professor, be a Professor in his/her own department.
  2. Have expertise in at least one of the candidate's research areas.
The Chair will inform the arm's-length reviewers of all pertinent facts regarding the candidate, with the candidate's representative publications, and will ask them for comments on
  • the quality of the candidate's research,
  • the candidate's research contributions to his/her research field,
  • the candidate's productivity relative to other academics at a similar stage in their career,
  • the candidate's potential as a research leader, and
  • the publication and review standards of the journals and conference proceedings in which the candidate has published, and their standings in the discipline.
3.5.1 Numbers of Arm's-Length Evaluators
  • For a second re-appointment for years 6 and 7 of tenure-track faculty, the Chair will contact 4 arm's-length evaluators for evaluation letters.
  • For tenure and promotion to Associate Professor, and promotion to Research Associate Professor, the Chair will contact 6 arm's-length evaluators.
  • For promotion to the rank of Professor or Research Professor, the Chair will contact 8 arm's-length evaluators.
3.5.2 The Selection Process
N below refers to the number of arm's-length evaluators that the Chair will contact for each type of greensheet reviews, as defined in Section 3.5.1.
  1. The candidate is asked to provide N nominations.
  2. The Chair compiles N other names from other sources.
  3. The Chair shows the N other names to the candidate to check whether the candidate has a conflict of interest or personal/professional disagreements with any of these names.
  4. The Chair selects and contacts N/2 names from the candidate's list, and N/2 names from the other list.

3.6 Faculty Input and Eligible Voters for RTP Reviews
3.6.1 Faculty Input and Schedule for RTP Reviews
The Chair should set an appropriate schedule for each greensheet review, so that the complete greensheets will be ready for faculty review at least 2 weeks before the submission deadline to the Dean's Office.

Once the greensheets are ready for faculty review, all faculty members, tenured and untenured (including tenure-track faculty, Lecturers, and Senior Lecturers) will be invited to review the greensheets and share their advice concerning the candidate with the Chair within a week. The feedback will be documented in the Chair's Evaluation.

At the beginning of the second week after the greensheets are complete, the Chair will convene a meeting of all eligible voters (as defined in Section 3.6.2) to discuss and vote on whether or not to recommend the candidate's application. This vote will be recorded in the Chair's Evaluation.

After the above faculty feedback and eligible voters' vote, the Chair will decide whether or not to recommend the candidate's application, and will inform the candidate with a detailed Chair's Evaluation.

3.6.2 Eligible Voters for RTP Reviews
When a candidate applies for promotion to a particular rank, only those faculty members who are already at this rank or above are eligible voters. When a candidate applies for reappointment at a particular rank, only those faculty members who have successfully passed their reappointment at this rank and will not be evaluated again for the same level of reappointment, are eligible voters.
  • For a Lecturer reappointment, only Senior Lecturers and tenured faculty members are eligible voters.
  • For a Senior Lecturer application, only Senior Lecturers and tenured faculty members are eligible voters.
  • For a Senior Lecturer reappointment, only tenured faculty members are eligible voters.
  • For a first tenure-track reappointment review, only tenured faculty members and those faculty who have successfully passed their first reappointment review are eligible voters.
  • For a second reappointment review, only tenured faculty members and those faculty who have successfully passed their second reappointment review are eligible voters.
  • For a tenure application, only tenured faculty members are eligible voters.
  • For a promotion application to Associate Professor, only Associate Professors and Professors are eligible voters.
  • For a promotion application to Professor, only Professors are eligible voters.
  • For a promotion application to Research Associate Professor, only Research Associate Professors, Research Professors, Associate Professors and Professors are eligible voters.
  • For a promotion application to Research Professor, only Professors and Research Professors are eligible voters.
The Chair is not an eligible voter. If the number of eligible voters is less than 3 for a particular type of greensheet review, the Chair will consult with the candidate(s) and invite our secondary appointments at appropriate ranks to make up at least 3 eligible voters for each greensheet review.

3.7 Guidelines for Promotion to Senior Lecturer

The following guidelines for promotion to Senior Lecturer were formally approved by Dean Robert Jenkins on May 29, 2003.

An application for Senior Lecturer in Computer Science will be evaluated on the following criteria.

  • A minimum of 6 years (within an eight year period) of service, as specified in the Union Contract
  • Subject to a regular RTP review without the research expectations
  • A good citizenship in terms of service activities within the Department
  • Evidence of sustained quality teaching

3.8 Tenure and Promotion to Associate Professor
3.8.1 Mandatory vs. Requested T&P Reviews
  • After 5 years' of service in a tenure-track position, a candidate for tenure and promotion will be notified by the Chair in the appropriate June that the review process must begin in the following academic year, and that the candidate is asked to nominate 6 arm's-length evaluators with a given deadline.
  • A faculty member may become a candidate at an earlier date. In such an early-tenure case, the faculty member should let the Chair know by the appropriate May. The Chair may provide some informal advice after possible consultation with tenured members in the Department, but the decision for early-tenure application is with the candidate.
  • The evaluation procedure is the same for both mandatory and requested reviews. A candidate for tenure and promotion in either case is expected to be an established researcher in his/her research field, in addition to the university criteria (see Appendix B).
3.8.2 Withdrawal of Early-Tenure Applications
After the Chair's Evaluation in Section 3.6.1, an early-tenure candidate may choose to withdraw the current tenure and promotion application.

3.9 Promotion to the Rank of Professor

An associate professor who wishes to be considered for promotion to the rank of Professor should notify the Chair by the appropriate May so that arm's-length evaluators can be organized in the following summer.

Promotion to the rank of Professor requires a sustained record of excellence in teaching, research, and service, and the candidate should enjoy a recognized national or international reputation in his/her research field.

After the Chair's Evaluation in Section 3.6.1, a candidate for promotion to Professor may choose to withdraw the current application.

3.10 Promotion to Research Associate Professor and Research Professor

A research faculty member may become a candidate for promotion by personal request or by recommendation of the Chair (who is the formal supervisor of each research faculty member in the Department). Such a request or recommendation should be discussed in the appropriate May so that arm's-length evaluators can be organized in the following summer.

Research faculty members do not have teaching or service expectations in the Department, but if the candidate has performed significant teaching or service work, he/she may choose to include this work in the promotion greensheets.

The candidate's research will be evaluated in a manner similar to that used by the Department in evaluating promotions to Associate Professor and Professor.

After the Chair's Evaluation in Section 3.6.1, a candidate for promotion may choose to withdraw the current application.

4. Annual Faculty Evaluations

In accordance with the Union Contract, the annual faculty review will focus on the faculty member's performance during the academic year in which the review takes place but will also take into account the faculty member's performance during the preceding two years.

In late January each year, each faculty member is requested to complete the Annual Faculty Activity Report form (see Appendix A below) by February 15. The faculty may choose to include any additional relevant information.

In April each year, the Chair will meet with the faculty member to discuss the faculty member's performance and the following academic year's workload plan. Before or at this meeting, the faculty member can add any written information to the faculty member's Annual Faculty Activity Report. The Chair will provide a written evaluation for the faculty member at or after the meeting.

4.1 Percentages of Faculty Effort

The percentages of effort devoted to each of the categories of teaching, research and scholarship, and service, are initially set in the annual April meeting between the Chair and the faculty member for returning faculty, and during the hiring process for new faculty. They are included in the faculty member's workload plan, and can be modified as appropriate or necessary during the following academic year.

The norms for these percentages are given below. 

Professor, Associate Professor and Assistant Professor

                Teaching:        40% 
                Research:        40% 
                Service:         20%        

Senior Lecturer, Lecturer

                Teaching:        80%                 
                Service:         20%        

Research Professor, Research Associate Professor and Research
Assistant Professor

                Research:       100%
The faculty member may wish to focus his/her energy on one particular activity in a particular year. In this case, the faculty member and the Chair should agree upon revised percentages at the annual April meeting and put them in the workload plan.

4.2 Chair's Evaluation on Annual Faculty Performance

Based on
  1. the workload plan for the current year,
  2. the Annual Faculty Activity Report,
  3. possible additional information from the faculty member before/at the annual April meeting, and
  4. course evaluations and student feedback in the past calendar year,
the Chair will evaluate the faculty member's performance in each of the categories of teaching, research and scholarship, and service, if its percentage in the workload plan is not zero. The evaluation will apply quality indicators in the University criteria (in Appendix B) and in the additional Computer Science specific descriptions in Section 3 to review the faculty member's annual performance.

The Chair will also provide one of the following 3 overall ratings on the faculty member's annual performance:

  • Outstanding. An outstanding performance must achieve at least two excellent ratings in teaching, research and scholarship, and service.
  • Unsatisfactory. An unsatisfactory performance would have at least one not acceptable rating and no excellent ratings in teaching, research and scholarship, and service.
  • Satisfactory. A satisfactory performance is neither outstanding nor unsatisfactory.

5. Rebuttal and Appeal

After receiving the Chair's Evaluation on either a RTP review or an annual performance review, the faculty member may rebut or appeal, following the procedures described in the Union Contract or the Officer's Handbook.
